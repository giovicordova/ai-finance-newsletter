### Pattern context
The channel's compute-financing thread keeps tightening. Monday it was SK Hynix raising ~$26.5B in a record US listing to fund memory fabs; Tuesday it was Helsing's $1.8B raise with Goldman, JPMorgan and a national pension fund backing AI-defence. Today the same money-into-compute story shows up on the demand side of the ledger: a model lab, Reflection AI, locking a multi-year, $1B+ contract for Nvidia capacity from a cloud provider — its second such deal in a month after SpaceX. The through-line is that access to Nvidia silicon is now being pre-committed years out and financed at billion-dollar scale by labs that are themselves flush with venture money. It also lands against this week's louder counter-current — the BIS warning (28 June, re-aired 14 July) that debt-financed AI capex and circular hyperscaler-lab stakes could turn the boom fragile — which is exactly the kind of arrangement deals like this one help build out.
