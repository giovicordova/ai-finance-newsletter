## Weekly Intelligence

**Theme 1 — The CPU-GPU split widens the bid**
For a year the trade has been "own Nvidia." This week it widened. Intel's Q1 put AI revenue at 60% of the book, growing 40%, and Xeon 6 inside NVIDIA's Rubin rack. AMD is up 45% month-to-date on a 60% annual data-centre growth plan. The CPU tier is no longer a roadkill hedge — it's a second bid. The implication: AI capex is not zero-sum between chip vendors; agentic workloads need host CPUs, custom ASICs and memory alongside GPUs. The open question is whether that second tier is accretive or whether it's Nvidia reselling silicon through partners.

**Theme 2 — The productivity dividend is landing as margin, not growth**
Snap cut 16% of staff and rallied 11%. Six US banks shed 15,000 roles in Q1 while booking record profits; BofA's Moynihan named AI as the reason for 1,000 of them. IBM's software AI ARR crossed $1.5B growing >40%. The pattern: firms are banking AI as cost-out, not redeploying it as top-line reinvestment. Clean for earnings, messy for macro. If the productivity gain shows up only in margins and never in net hiring, payroll softens into Q3 and the curve has to reprice. Current Fed pricing doesn't reflect it.

**Theme 3 — AI-infrastructure debt goes industrial**
A $5.7B record junk print for Google-linked data centres with $19B of orders. PIMCO weighing $14B for Oracle's Michigan campus. QTS drew $12.5B of demand on a Microsoft-tied bond. Mistral raised $830M in debt alone. EQT seeded an AI Infrastructure strategy with a 100GW pipeline. The $4T five-year build is being financed not by hyperscaler balance sheets but by capital markets digesting structured AI-infra paper. The shape is starting to rhyme with the late-1990s telecom build — same asset-heavy thesis, same debt stack, same unanswered question about terminal value.

**What Changed**
The consensus at the start of the week was "MSFT and GOOGL on Apr 29 decide the cycle." By Friday, Intel's beat and Anthropic's $30B ARR pass on OpenAI's $25B had front-run the question. Two shifts: AI revenue is showing up on non-obvious income statements — IBM software, Intel DCAI, BofA productivity — making next week's hyperscaler print a confirmation, not a verdict. And the frontier-lab race has diverged. Anthropic's capital efficiency is now the bear case against OpenAI, not merely a stat.
