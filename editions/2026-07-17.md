### Stories considered
- **TSMC's record Q2 2026 profit — chosen for the news post.** Reported Wednesday 16 July 2026 (Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company's own results release, filed with the US SEC as a Form 6-K — the primary source; corroborated by Reuters via Yahoo Finance/AOL, Bloomberg, Taipei Times, Motley Fool, Benzinga and others). TSMC reported consolidated Q2 revenue of NT$1,270.38 billion (US$40.20 billion, +36.0% YoY / +33.7% in USD) and net income of NT$706.56 billion (+77.4% YoY), a record, with diluted EPS of NT$27.25 (US$4.31 per ADR). Gross margin was 67.7%, net profit margin 55.6%. On the earnings call management also raised full-year 2026 capital-spending guidance to US$60–64 billion (from US$52–56 billion), lifted its 2026 revenue-growth outlook to "slightly more than 40%" (from 30%-plus), and pledged an additional US$100 billion for its Arizona factories (bringing the state total to ~US$265 billion), all tied to accelerating AI demand. Shares fell ~4.6% premarket as investors focused on the capital-spending ramp. This is the freshest, most money-moving, cleanly primary-sourceable AI-finance beat in the 24-hour window and was explicitly flagged as tomorrow's natural lead in the 16 July edition — the reported net profit is the genuinely new number (Q2 revenue was disclosed with Monday's monthly sales report and already logged as a preview runner-up on 15 and 16 July). **The news post links the SEC 6-K primary and sticks to what that filing supports** — the record profit, the +77% net-income jump, the +36% revenue growth, and the ~US$40 billion revenue. ### Pattern context
Friday closes a week in which the AI build-out's demand stopped being a projection and became reported, audited numbers from the two suppliers that gate the entire chain. Wednesday, ASML — the near-monopoly maker of the machines that print advanced chips — raised its full-year sales forecast ~16% and added ~30% EUV capacity. Today, Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company, the foundry that turns those machines into chips, posted a record profit (+77% net income) and, on the call, raised its 2026 capital-spending plan to US$60–64B and pledged another US$100B for Arizona. Both are the demand-side evidence the week's louder counter-current — the BIS warning re-aired 14 July, plus visible bond-market fatigue (AI bond issuance past US$218B, SpaceX/Meta bonds trading wide) — has been pressing on. The tell that the debate is live: TSMC's own shares fell ~4.6% not on weak results but on the size of its spending plan.
