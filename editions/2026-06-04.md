### Pattern context
Four days of the AI-finance flywheel rotating through its parts: supply (Nvidia Vera Rubin in production, 1 June), capital (Alphabet's $80B equity raise + Anthropic's confidential S-1, 1–2 June), demand (HPE's $1.8B fresh AI-server orders / $16.4B cumulative, 3 June), and now exit (Quantinuum prints the first traditional quantum-computing IPO, sized above range at $60 for $1.68B). Sitting alongside is the debt side: Apollo and Blackstone's still-marketing $36B private credit package to fund Google TPU buys for Anthropic — the mirror image of Alphabet's $80B equity raise, with Broadcom backstopping the senior tranches via its TPU co-design role. Recurring names: Honeywell, Quantinuum, Anthropic, Alphabet, Google, Apollo, Blackstone, Broadcom, JP Morgan, Morgan Stanley, Hewlett Packard Enterprise, Nvidia. Live threads going into Friday's weekly take: (1) AI-financing capital stack visibly bifurcating — public equity (Alphabet, Quantinuum, soon Anthropic) on one side, balance-sheet-backed private credit (Apollo/Blackstone/Broadcom-Anthropic) on the other; (2) the AI-IPO window is now open and the queue (Anthropic confidential S-1 just filed, Cognition $1B at $26B private, OpenAI listing timeline) is forming behind Quantinuum; (3) the demand-side print at HPE plus Goldman's "not a straight line" caveat give the first hint of a hyperscaler-vs-enterprise speed gap; (4) C3.ai's -53% YoY revenue drop is the counter-example — not every "AI-pure-play" name is riding the same flywheel.
