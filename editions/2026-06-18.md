### Pattern context
Today's beat moves the week's dominant thread — how the AI compute build-out is being financed and who it pays — one rung down the supply chain. The past two weeks ran through equity (SpaceX's record IPO and >$2T debut) and jumbo debt (Nvidia's $25B bond, three-times oversubscribed). Today it shows up as demand landing on a picks-and-shovels name: Jabil, which assembles AI server hardware, raised its AI-related revenue outlook to ~$13.6 billion (≈50% growth) and lifted full-year guidance — the build-out's spending becoming someone else's booked revenue. Running alongside is SpaceX's $60B all-stock purchase of Cursor's parent (announced Tuesday, missed in our 16–17 editions), which uses freshly-public equity to buy into AI coding and pits SpaceX directly against Anthropic and OpenAI. Recurring names: Nvidia, SpaceX/xAI, OpenAI, Anthropic, Marvell, Hewlett Packard Enterprise, Jabil. Live threads into Friday's take: (1) build-out capex converting into supplier earnings (Jabil, HPE, Marvell); (2) the AI M&A turn — newly-public AI-adjacent equity (SpaceX) being spent on AI software; (3) the IPO/debt pipeline (OpenAI/Anthropic S-1s, more AI names tapping credit); (4) a macro cross-current as the Fed signals a possible late-2026 rate hike.
