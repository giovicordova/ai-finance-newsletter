### Stories considered
- **ASML raises full-year 2026 guidance on AI chip demand — chosen for the news post.** Reported Wednesday 15 July 2026 (ASML's own press release, primary; carried on GlobeNewswire and corroborated by eeNews Europe, Global Banking & Finance, The Elec, Investing.com and others). ASML Holding NV — the Dutch company that holds a near-monopoly on the extreme-ultraviolet (EUV) lithography machines used to print the most advanced logic and memory chips — reported Q2 total net sales of €9.3 billion (≈€9.33B) and net income of €2.9 billion, with quarterly net bookings of roughly €5.5 billion (above the ~€4.4–5.3B analysts expected). Crucially, it raised its full-year 2026 net sales guidance to €43–45 billion, up sharply from its prior €36–40 billion range (≈16% higher at the midpoint), and said it is adding ~30% to its 2026 low-NA EUV capacity for 2027. Management tied the stronger outlook to accelerating AI-driven demand for advanced logic and memory chips. This is the freshest, most money-moving AND cleanly free-primary-sourceable AI-finance beat in the 24-hour window: a guidance raise from the single most important chip-equipment supplier is genuinely new information (not just an earnings beat) and speaks directly to the channel's running question of whether AI capital spending is sustainable. The post sticks to what the primary release supports — the raised €43–45B full-year forecast, the €9.3B Q2 sales, and the AI-demand framing. The €2.9B net income, the ~€5.5B bookings, the prior €36–40B range, the 30% capacity add, and the Intel High-NA EUV shipment milestone are left to the Notes to keep the post tight and under the cap.

### Pattern context
The channel's AI build-out thread keeps climbing the supply chain, and today it reaches the pick-and-shovel layer. Recent editions ran on securing raw silicon (Micron, 10 July), raising public capital against memory demand (SK Hynix's record $26.5B Nasdaq listing, 13 July), financing AI-defence (Helsing, 14 July) and pre-committing Nvidia compute (Nebius/Reflection, 15 July). ASML now raises its full-year forecast ~16% and adds ~30% EUV capacity — the equipment supplier that gates all of the above confirming, in hard guidance, the demand those stories only implied. It also lands squarely on the week's louder counter-current: the BIS warning (re-aired 14 July) that AI capital spending could be running too hot. A near-monopoly supplier raising guidance and expanding capacity is exactly the demand-side evidence that debate turns on — which is why it edges out a fresher but smaller application-layer beat (Emergent's $130M unicorn round) and a same-day debt-market story (QTS's $3.25B loan) that could only be sourced behind a paywall.
