### Stories considered
- **Nvidia's $20B bond sale — chosen for the news post.** On 15 June Nvidia began marketing at least $20 billion of investment-grade corporate bonds — its first debt issuance since June 2021, when it raised $5 billion. The deal spans seven tranches maturing from roughly 2 to 30 years (longest notes maturing 2056), with the 30-year tranche initially marketed at about 0.9 percentage points over comparable US Treasuries. Proceeds are for general corporate purposes including repayment and refinancing of existing debt. JPMorgan Chase, Morgan Stanley and Goldman Sachs are among the lead banks. This is the single most material AI-finance market event in the window: it puts the most profitable company of the AI boom into the debt market for the first time during that boom, joining Alphabet, Amazon and Meta in funding the build-out with borrowed money. Cross-corroborated across CNBC, Yahoo Finance/Bloomberg, US News and Gurufocus snippets; CNBC (allowlisted, free) is the news-post link. ### Pattern context
Today's beat tightens the dominant thread of the past two weeks: how the AI compute build-out is being financed. Last week's editions tracked it through equity (SpaceX's record $75B IPO and +19% debut) and through data-centre leases (Applied Digital's ~$36B backlog). Today it reaches the debt market — and not at a model lab or a data-centre landlord, but at Nvidia itself, the most profitable company of the AI boom, borrowing $20B for the first time since 2021. The interesting wrinkle for the Friday take: even Nvidia, awash in cash, is now choosing to fund with borrowed money alongside Alphabet, Amazon and Meta, which means the build-out's appetite for capital is outrunning even the sector's best balance sheet. Recurring names: Nvidia, Alphabet, Amazon, Meta, SpaceX/xAI, OpenAI, Applied Digital.
