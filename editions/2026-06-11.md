### Pattern context
Today's Oracle print is the first hard revenue-side confirmation of the demand pattern the rest of the week's editions have been mapping on the supply side. Monday's beat (Trump–OpenAI public-stake talks) framed political capital flowing toward AI labs; Tuesday's (Apple WWDC Siri AI on Gemini) framed Apple licensing a brain rather than building one; Wednesday's (Broadcom / Apollo / Blackstone $35B SPV closing for Anthropic) printed the private-credit funding leg with Broadcom as a structural counterparty. Today the customer side prints: Oracle's backlog of contracted future cloud revenue jumped $85B sequentially in a single quarter, to $638B — 363% above year-ago, with $75B of that customer-funded directly through prepaid or supplied GPUs. The headline read for an AI-finance reader is that the demand isn't just being marketed at AI infrastructure providers; customers (Stargate participants, hyperscalers, frontier labs) are now pre-funding the chip side of the contracts themselves, which substantially de-risks the build-out for Oracle but also reframes who's actually carrying the chip-capex risk in 2026 — increasingly the customer, not the cloud. The unstated tension that broke the stock ~7% after-hours: even with $75B customer-funded, Oracle still needs to raise an additional ~$40B (debt + equity) to fund its own data-centre build-out, against $55.7B FY26 capex that overshot its own $50B guide. Recurring names this week: Oracle, OpenAI, Anthropic, Apple, Google, Broadcom, Apollo, Blackstone, SpaceX, Sam Altman, Trump, Larry Ellison, Tim Cook (exiting). Worth watching for the rest of the week: SpaceX prices tonight after the US close, debuts Friday — the capital-markets event that closes the week. Friday's weekly take will set the week's four financing models against each other: Apple-licences-Gemini as op-ex ($1B/year), Anthropic-leases-TPUs-via-private-credit ($35B Broadcom-backstopped SPV), OpenAI-becomes-quasi-public (S-1 + Trump stake), Oracle-takes-customer-prepaid-chip-deposits (Stargate-style $75B+ funded by counterparty) — four different ways the AI capex stack is being absorbed without diluting the equity holders of the model lab itself.
