### Stories considered
- **Microsoft to deploy AMD's Helios rack-scale AI system on Azure — chosen for the news post.** Announced 20 July 2026 via AMD's own newsroom (primary, allowlisted under company IR/official blogs, free). Microsoft and Advanced Micro Devices expanded their long-term strategic partnership spanning GPUs, CPUs, networking and software on Microsoft Azure. At its centre, Microsoft will deploy the AMD Helios Rackscale Solution to power frontier-model AI inference for Microsoft and its Azure AI customers. Helios combines AMD Instinct MI455X GPUs, AMD EPYC "Venice" CPUs, Pensando networking and ROCm software in an open, integrated rack-scale platform, and is the first rack-scale rival to Nvidia's Grace Blackwell and Vera Rubin systems. Azure will also add two new virtual-machine series (HDv2 for agentic AI and data pipelines, HXv2 for chip design and technical computing) built on 6th-gen EPYC "Venice" processors. AMD begins shipping Helios to customers, Microsoft included, in the second half of 2026. This is the freshest, largest, cleanly free-primary-sourceable AI-infrastructure story in the 24-hour window: a major hyperscaler committing to AMD's flagship AI rack, landing the day before AMD's "Advancing AI 2026" event (22–23 July) that yesterday's edition flagged as a week-ahead test. **The news post links the AMD primary and sticks to what it clearly supports** — the expanded partnership, Microsoft running Helios on Azure, and the second-half-2026 shipping timeline — while holding the chip-level detail (MI455X, Venice, Pensando, the new VM series) and the competitive Nvidia framing in these Notes.
