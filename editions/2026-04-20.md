**Alibaba draws the analyst pile-on** - BofA pegged the name "one of the most compelling AI plays in China" at $172 (19% upside) after the HappyHorse video model; March-quarter AI capex nearly doubled to $2.93B. [Source](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/19/baba-makes-ai-investments-stock-analysts-want-to-see.html)

**Alphabet capex set to nearly double** - Ahead of April 29 results, Alphabet's guided 2026 capex of $175-185B sits against $91.4B spent in 2025; Street wants Cloud >50% y/y to justify it. [Source](https://www.ig.com/ae/news-and-trade-ideas/alphabet-q1-2026-earnings-preview-260420)

**Hiro Finance goes dark today** - The OpenAI-acquired personal-finance app sunsets April 20 with data deletion May 15, clearing the deck for OpenAI's own vertical finance product. [Source](https://techcrunch.com/2026/04/13/openai-has-bought-ai-personal-finance-startup-hiro/)

**Huang pushes back on China curbs** - Nvidia's CEO warned at the weekend that US export controls risk splitting the global AI ecosystem and accelerating Chinese independence, as regulators weigh the MATCH Act. [Source](https://www.indrastra.com/2026/04/nvidia-ceo-defends-chip-sales-to-china.html)

## One Contrarian Thought

Private credit keeps getting singled out as the AI-disruption fall guy: $500B lent to software firms whose moats are eroding. Bloomberg Opinion flipped the frame last Thursday. Private equity is worse off, because it owns the equity while private credit holds the senior debt. If the AI-disruption thesis is right, the risk getting repriced is on the wrong side of the cap stack.
