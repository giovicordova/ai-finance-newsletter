### Pattern context
This week is sequencing the AI capital stack's three structural set-pieces. Monday's Musk-v-OpenAI verdict cleared the legal overhang on the OpenAI for-profit conversion and the ~$500B–$850B private-valuation/committed-capital stack sitting against it. Today's beat — the Blackstone–Google TPU joint venture — fills in the supply-side counter-move that yesterday's edition didn't have room for: Google monetising TPUs through a Blackstone-led, majority-private-equity-owned vehicle that sells the chips as compute-as-a-service separate from Google Cloud, with ~$25B of total compute investment (incl. leverage) targeted around the first 500 MW in 2027. Tomorrow Nvidia's FY27 Q1 print (consensus ~$78B revenue, ~10% options-implied move, data centre ~$73B per the previews flagged yesterday) tests the demand side. The week's framing is therefore tightening from a single name-and-overhang story (OpenAI/Musk) into a three-pillar capital-stack picture: legal overhang resolved (Mon), TPU-as-merchant-cloud established (today), Nvidia demand cadence pending (Thu). Names recurring across the archive: Blackstone (data-centre footprint thread), Google/Alphabet, Nvidia, Anthropic (the $30B/$900B raise still pending), OpenAI, Microsoft.
