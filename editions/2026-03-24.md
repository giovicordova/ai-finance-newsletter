── DEEP DIVE ───────────────────────────
Micron's results confirm what the GPU story has been telling us: AI infrastructure demand is broadening beyond chips to every component in the stack. HBM is now the binding constraint for AI inference performance — you can have all the GPUs you want, but without sufficient high-bandwidth memory, they sit idle. The sold-out-through-2027 guidance means cloud providers planning next-generation AI inference clusters need to lock in memory contracts now or risk delays. For financial firms building proprietary AI infrastructure, this adds yet another procurement headache to a supply chain already strained by CPU and GPU constraints.

── ONE CONTRARIAN THOUGHT ──────────────
The AI model insurance market is a clever financial innovation built on a fragile foundation. Actuaries have decades of data to price fire, flood, and auto risk. They have essentially zero historical data on AI model failure rates at scale. The $2B market is pricing risk based on vibes and first principles, not statistical evidence. The first major AI model failure that triggers a wave of claims will test whether these products are insurance or speculation.
