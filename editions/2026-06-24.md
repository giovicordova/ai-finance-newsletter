### Pattern context
The cycle's dominant thread all month has been how the AI compute build-out is financed and who books the revenue: equity (SpaceX's record listing, 15 June; Alphabet's ~$84B raise), jumbo debt (Nvidia's $25B bond, 16–17 June; Anthropic's ~$36B TPU financing), supplier earnings (Jabil, 18 June), the first demand-side wrinkle (Accenture's softer bookings, 19 June), passive flows (the Nasdaq-100 adding CoreWeave, Nebius, Astera Labs and Teradyne, 22 June), and the picks-and-shovels layer (Dell and Super Micro Computer's Vera Rubin server launches, 23 June). Today the thread turns: the same names that have been mechanically bought all year met their first broad valuation reckoning. South Korea's KOSPI — roughly half of which is SK Hynix and Samsung Electronics — fell 8.1% and tripped circuit breakers twice as investors questioned whether hyperscalers can sustain AI-hardware spending, dragging Micron ~11% in U.S. hours ahead of its own print. Recurring names: SK Hynix, Samsung, Micron, Nvidia, the AI-infrastructure complex. Live into the rest of the week: Micron's fiscal Q3 print (24 June, after the close) and Qualcomm's Investor Day data-centre disclosures (24 June) as the next two reads.
