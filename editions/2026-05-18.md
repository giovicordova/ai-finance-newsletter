- Musk v. Altman jury deliberations begin Monday May 18 in Oakland federal court — chosen for news post. Three-week trial concluded its liability phase with closing arguments on Thu May 14; nine-person jury (six women, three men) begins deliberating today; remedies phase before Judge Yvonne Gonzalez Rogers also begins today. Musk's $38M in 2015-2017 donations to OpenAI as a nonprofit are at the heart of the dispute; lawyers in January quantified damages at up to $134B from OpenAI and Microsoft as "wrongful gains." ### Pattern context
This week's thread shifts register. The past two weeks were saturated with capacity-stack prints (Cerebras IPO, Applied Materials Q2, Cisco AI orders, Tencent capex). Today the focus moves from how big the buildout is to whether the legal foundation underneath the largest AI lab in the world holds. The Musk v. Altman jury deliberations put OpenAI's ~$500B for-profit conversion — and by extension the >$130B of investor capital from Microsoft, SoftBank, Amazon and Nvidia that has been committed against that conversion — into a fact-finding posture for the first time. Even though the verdict is advisory and Judge Gonzalez Rogers has signaled she will likely follow it, the structural remedies on the table (ouster of Altman and Brockman, unwinding of the 2025 recap) are unprecedented. Wednesday's Nvidia Q1 print is the next major capacity-side datapoint; today's deliberations are the governance counterweight to that thread.
