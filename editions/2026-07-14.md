### Pattern context
Last week the channel's dominant thread was the AI build-out's binding constraint sinking down the physical stack — power (Anthropic / TeraWulf), then silicon (Micron), then a record memory listing (SK Hynix). Today's beat rotates the lens from the physical inputs to the capital pool feeding AI more broadly: mainstream financial institutions — Goldman Sachs, JPMorgan, a national pension fund — writing a $1.8B growth cheque into AI-defence at an $18B valuation. It rhymes with the running "AI funding firehose is still open" runner-ups (Together AI, Baseten, Norm AI) but is an order of magnitude larger and led by balance-sheet institutional money rather than venture funds alone. It also lands against Monday's cross-current — the SK Hynix pullback and the AI-profitability debate — a useful reminder that even as private AI valuations climb, public markets are starting to press on when the spending pays off.
