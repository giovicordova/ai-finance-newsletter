### Pattern context
Last week the thread was capacity: $725B of 2026 hyperscaler capex, custom silicon eating Nvidia's rotation, Anthropic at $30B ARR weighing $900B, Berkshire's Greg Abel quantifying data-center peak load on the utility side. Today's beat moves the conversation from supply (chips, megawatts) and capital (frontier-lab valuations) to distribution. Anthropic and OpenAI did not sign a single bank or single PE house — they each lined up a bench of them, valued in the billions, with the explicit goal of pushing Claude and GPT directly into the operating businesses owned by Apollo, Blackstone, KKR-style buyout portfolios. That bypasses the McKinsey / Accenture / Deloitte deployment layer the consulting firms have spent two years trying to monetise. Goldman Sachs as an Anthropic founding partner is the headline asymmetry; SoftBank and Bain showing up on OpenAI's side is more continuity than surprise. Palantir's blowout print — US commercial +133%, FY26 guide +71% — lands the same day as evidence that PE-portfolio-style deployment is the revenue engine, not a hypothesis. Tonight's AMD print is the next scheduled risk to the capacity leg of the thread.
