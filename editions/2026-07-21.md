### Pattern context
The channel has spent the last week-plus almost entirely on the AI-infrastructure build-out — the chip-supplier layer (SK Hynix, ASML, TSMC) and then the software/data layer (Databricks' $188B private mark on 20 July). Today's Hut 8 lease pulls the thread back to physical capacity: a single high-investment-grade tenant is now paying $9.8 billion to fully book a one-gigawatt Texas campus, taking campus-level committed value to $19.6 billion. That is real, contracted, long-dated demand for AI compute — a counterpoint to the recurring bubble/sustainability worry the channel has tracked (BIS warnings, AI bond-market fatigue). The next test is imminent: Alphabet reports Q2 on 22 July with $190B of 2026 capex guidance on the line, and AMD's Advancing AI event runs 22–23 July. Both will show whether the demand these leases assume is actually being funded at the top of the stack.
