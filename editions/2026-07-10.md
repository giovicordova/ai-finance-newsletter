### Stories considered
- **Micron's up-to-$3B US semiconductor supply-chain investment — chosen for the news post.** Announced Thursday 9 July 2026 (GlobeNewswire primary; carried by Reuters, HPCwire, StockTitan, Semiconductor Digest, Yahoo Finance, Manila Times). Micron Technology (Nasdaq: MU) said it will invest up to $3 billion to strengthen the US semiconductor supply-chain ecosystem. The centrepiece: $500 million of strategic financing to GlobalWafers Co. to advance its GlobalWafers America 300mm raw silicon wafer manufacturing facility in Sherman, Texas, paired with a 10-year supply agreement giving Micron access to significant US-made wafer capacity. Micron framed the move as securing a reliable domestic supply of critical manufacturing materials to support surging demand for advanced memory and storage driven by artificial intelligence. The company also raised its cumulative planned US investment to more than $250 billion through 2035 (up from $200B a year earlier). Shares rose roughly 6–7% on the day. This is the freshest, hardest, most money-moving AI-finance beat in the 24-hour window, and it advances the channel's running constraint thread one layer deeper — from power (last week's National Grid / Joulent and Monday's Anthropic / TeraWulf lease) down to the raw silicon the chips themselves are printed on. The post sticks to the claims the primary release supports — the up-to-$3B figure, the $500M to GlobalWafers, the Sherman, Texas wafer plant, the 10-year supply deal, and the AI-demand framing. The $250B/2035 cumulative figure and the exact share move are left to the Notes to keep the post tight.

### Weekly threads (Friday only)
- **The scarce input keeps sinking down the stack.** Over the past two weeks the AI build-out's binding constraint has moved from money to ever more physical inputs: last Friday it was electricity (National Grid Ventures' $1.75B for Joulent, co-locating power plants at the data-centre fence to bypass the grid); Monday it was 20 years of physical megawatts (Anthropic's ~$19B TeraWulf lease); and Friday it is the raw silicon itself (Micron's up-to-$3B move to lock US wafer supply). Links: [3 Jul](https://github.com/giovicordova/ai-finance-newsletter/blob/main/editions/2026-07-03.md), [9 Jul](https://github.com/giovicordova/ai-finance-newsletter/blob/main/editions/2026-07-09.md), [10 Jul](https://github.com/giovicordova/ai-finance-newsletter/blob/main/editions/2026-07-10.md).
- **Multi-decade lock-ins on physical supply.** The vehicle keeps being a long-dated contract that ties up a real-world input for years: Anthropic's 20-year data-centre lease (9 Jul) and Micron's 10-year GlobalWafers wafer-supply agreement (10 Jul). The financing has stopped chasing valuations and started chasing certainty of physical supply.

### Pattern context
This week continues, and deepens, the thread last Friday's edition opened: the AI build-out's binding constraint has shifted from capital to physical inputs. On 3 July the National Grid / Joulent deal named electricity as the scarcity; Monday's Anthropic / TeraWulf lease locked two decades of physical power onto a converted mining site; and Friday's Micron beat descends one layer further, to the raw silicon wafers the chips are printed on. The recurring shape is a well-capitalised buyer signing a long-dated contract (10–20 years) to guarantee supply of a physical input, not to chase a valuation.
