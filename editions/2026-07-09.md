### Stories considered
- **Anthropic's 20-year, ~$19B lease with TeraWulf — chosen for the news post.** Announced Monday 6 July (TeraWulf IR press release; covered by CNBC, Reuters, DataCenterDynamics, CoinDesk). TeraWulf Inc. (Nasdaq: WULF), a former bitcoin-mining company converting its power sites to AI infrastructure, signed a 20-year lease with Anthropic for a purpose-built campus at its Justified Data site in Hawesville, Kentucky. The lease is expected to generate ~$19 billion of contracted lease revenue over the initial term, supported by an investment-grade credit backstop. The campus will accommodate ~401 MW of critical IT load, developed in phases, with initial capacity in H2 2027 ramping to full 401 MW by early 2028. In the same announcement TeraWulf agreed to sell its 50.1% stake in the 168-MW Abernathy, Texas joint venture to an investor group led by Fluidstack, monetising a ~$450M investment at a premium. This is the freshest, hardest, most money-moving AI-finance beat in the window and it advances the channel's running power-constraint thread: after last Friday's National Grid / Joulent deal (funding generation at the data-centre fence), a frontier lab is now locking two decades of compute capacity onto a converted power site. The post sticks to the claims the primary release supports — the 20-year term, the ~$19B revenue, Hawesville KY, ~401 MW, and the late-2027 in-service date. The Abernathy/Fluidstack leg and the credit-backstop detail are left to the Notes to keep the post tight.
