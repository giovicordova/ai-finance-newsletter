### Pattern context
This week ran two threads in parallel and tightened both. On the capacity side: AMD's data-center segment +57% to $5.8B, Nvidia underwriting three new Corning fiber plants for up to $3.2B, Arm's data-center royalties more than doubling YoY, CoreWeave printing $2.078B Q1 revenue against a $99.4B backlog with $2B fresh from Nvidia and an $8.5B credit facility. Capacity is no longer being procured at one layer of the stack — it is being secured at silicon, photonics, royalties, and DC capacity simultaneously, with the buyer side directly capitalising the supplier side. On the distribution side: Anthropic and OpenAI both stood up PE-backed services firms on Monday to push frontier models into ~2,000 portfolio operating companies; on Tuesday Anthropic shipped 10 finance-specific agents into Microsoft 365 / Moody's / FIS as the workflow layer; today Cloudflare became the first profitable, growing public company to cut 20% of headcount with the explicit framing that the company itself is being rebuilt around agents. The week's signal isn't whether AI is real, or whether the buildout is real — both questions are now closed at multiple layers. The signal is that the unit of deployment is moving from "AI feature inside an existing product" to "AI as the operating model the company runs on." Block and Oracle had already cut headcount citing AI; Cloudflare is the first to do it from a position of beat-and-cut rather than miss-and-cut.
