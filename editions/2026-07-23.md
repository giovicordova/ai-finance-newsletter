### Pattern context
For two straight weeks the channel has run on the AI build-out from the bottom up: the chip-supplier layer (SK Hynix, ASML, TSMC), the software/data layer (Databricks' $188B mark), physical capacity (Hut 8's $19.6B Beacon Point leases), and the compute layer's competitive structure (Microsoft running AMD's Helios rack on Azure). Yesterday's edition flagged Alphabet's Q2 as the week's key test of whether that demand is actually funded at the top of the stack. It now is: Alphabet raised 2026 capex guidance to as much as $205 billion — above its own prior range — with Google Cloud up 82% and the CFO calling compute "supply-constrained." The one caution the market registered is that even beats now come with a bigger spending bill (shares fell after hours). AMD's Advancing AI event today and the wider AI-debt-issuance figure (~$489B YTD, per Goldman) are the next threads on how all this capacity gets built and paid for.
