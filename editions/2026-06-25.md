### Stories considered
- **Micron's fiscal Q3 2026 record earnings — chosen for the news post.** After the U.S. close on Wednesday 24 June, Micron Technology reported results for the quarter ended 28 May 2026: record revenue of $41.46 billion, versus $23.86 billion the prior quarter and $9.30 billion a year earlier — a year-over-year increase of roughly 346% (more than four times). GAAP net income was $28.24 billion ($24.67 per diluted share); non-GAAP net income $28.86 billion ($25.11 per diluted share), beating the ~$20.28 consensus and extending Micron's run of earnings beats to seven straight quarters. Gross margin came in above 81%. Operating cash flow was $25.39 billion; the board declared a $0.15-per-share quarterly dividend. For fiscal Q4 2026 Micron guided revenue of $50.0 billion ± $1.0 billion and non-GAAP EPS of $31.00 ± $1.00. This is the cleanest, most material, genuinely-fresh AI-finance print in the 24-hour window — flagged in the last three editions as the midweek AI-memory read, and now the receipts for the whole month's AI-build-out thread. The news post sticks to claims the SEC 8-K supports — record $41.5B revenue, the $9.3B year-ago base (more than four times), and the ~$50B next-quarter guide — and omits the EPS detail, the 81% gross-margin figure and the share-price reaction to keep the post tight and strictly factual. https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/0000723125/000072312526000013/a2026q3ex991-pressrelease.htm
- **Qualcomm Investor Day — Dragonfly data-centre roadmap and Meta as named customer — strong runner-up.** At its 24 June Investor Day in New York, Qualcomm unveiled its Dragonfly data-centre portfolio (the Dragonfly C1000 central processor, High Bandwidth Compute, and the Dragonfly AI300 inference accelerator joining the AI200 and AI250 in an annual roadmap). The headline money/positioning fact: Meta Platforms entered a multi-generational agreement to use Qualcomm's Dragonfly C1000 processor across its data centres, with the C1000 expected to be available in 2028; the AI200 is on track for initial customer shipments later this year. A genuinely-fresh, in-window beat and a clean primary (Business Wire), but Micron's record print is the larger, more material money-mover and the more direct culmination of the week's thread, so Qualcomm is the runner-up. https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20260624731900/en/Qualcomm-Unveils-Comprehensive-Data-Center-Roadmap-for-the-Agentic-AI-Era-with-New-Qualcomm-Dragonfly-Portfolio
