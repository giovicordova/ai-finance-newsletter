- Broadcom / Apollo / Blackstone "AI XPV Platform" closes $35B initial tranche for Anthropic — chosen for news post. Announced via joint press release on Tuesday 9 June 2026 (US morning) — outside yesterday's Europe/Rome briefing window. ### Pattern context
The Anthropic / Broadcom / Apollo / Blackstone close is the closing chapter of live thread #1 from last Friday's Notes and folds the week's pattern together neatly. Last Friday's edition framed the bifurcating capital stack — Alphabet's $80B public-equity raise funding hyperscaler capex on one side; the Apollo / Blackstone private-credit SPV with Broadcom backstop funding lab-side compute leases on the other. Today the second side prints at scale, with two additions that escalate it past a single SPV: (1) the senior $30B is explicitly Broadcom-residual-value-backed and only the $4.5B junior tranche carries unsecured risk — i.e., the chip vendor is now a structural counterparty in private credit, not just a supplier; (2) the platform is sized to 20+ GW through 2028 and explicitly extends to OpenAI, meaning this is not one deal but the template for an asset class. Recurring names this week: Anthropic, Broadcom, Apollo, Blackstone, Google, Apple, OpenAI, Trump, Sam Altman, SpaceX, Nvidia, Fluidstack. The third pattern point is Google's $920M/month SpaceX compute lease (disclosed via SpaceX's IPO FWP) — Google is simultaneously (a) funding Apple's Siri intelligence via the Gemini licence, (b) the chip designer behind Anthropic's leased TPUs through the Broadcom co-design, and (c) renting GPUs from SpaceX through 2029 — a triangulated position that makes Google a counterparty to all three of the week's biggest compute-financing prints. Worth watching for the rest of the week: SpaceX prices Thursday after the US close, debuts Friday — the capital-markets event that closes the week and the natural news beat for Friday's edition. Friday's weekly take will set the three financing models side by side: Apple-licences-Gemini ($1B/year op-ex), Anthropic-leases-TPUs-via-private-credit ($35B debt SPV, Broadcom-backstopped), Google-rents-GPUs-from-SpaceX ($30B contract through 2029).
