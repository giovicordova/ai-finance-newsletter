### Stories considered
- **SK Hynix's $26.5B US listing on Nasdaq — chosen for the news post.** Priced Thursday 9 July, debuted Friday 10 July (CNBC primary interview and debut coverage; corroborated by Bloomberg, TechCrunch, Al Jazeera, Fortune, Yahoo Finance, Quartz). SK Hynix Inc. sold 177.9 million American depositary shares at $149 each, raising ~$26.5 billion — the largest US share sale ever by a foreign issuer (surpassing Alibaba's $25B 2014 debut) and the second-largest US share sale overall behind SpaceX's ~$86B. The stock (ticker SKHYV, switching to SKHY on Tuesday) closed its first session ~13% higher at $168.01. Chairman told CNBC "demand is enormous" for the high-bandwidth memory that feeds AI accelerators; proceeds fund new fabs and EUV lithography equipment. This is the freshest and most money-moving AI-finance capital-markets beat live on a Monday — the weekend was quiet — and it hasn't been covered in the channel (Friday's edition was Micron's wafer-supply move). It extends the running memory/silicon-scarcity thread from the demand-financing side: a memory maker tapping US public markets for ~$26.5B to expand capacity. The post sticks to what the CNBC debut coverage supports — the $26.5B raise, the record framing, the $149 pricing, the ~13% first-day rise, and the AI-memory-demand citation. ### Pattern context
The memory/silicon thread that ran through last week stays dominant, but shifts from securing supply to raising capital against demand. On 10 July the channel led with Micron locking a decade of US wafer supply; today a rival memory maker, SK Hynix, taps US public markets for a record ~$26.5B to fund fab and lithography expansion. The through-line across both is high-bandwidth memory as the AI build-out's tightest, most valuable input — last week the story was guaranteeing its raw materials, this week it is the scale of capital that memory demand can now command. The weekend was quiet, so Friday's blockbuster listing is the freshest live beat on Monday. Runner-ups (Norm AI $120M, EDX Markets $76M) confirm the private AI-fintech firehose is still open but none is fresh or large enough to lead against a record public listing.
