### Stories considered
- Musk v. Altman jury verdict (Monday May 18) — chosen for news post. The advisory nine-person jury unanimously found Musk's claims barred by the three-year statute of limitations after less than two hours of deliberation. Musk filed in February 2024; jury found he was aware of OpenAI's conduct as early as 2021. Judge Yvonne Gonzalez Rogers immediately adopted the verdict, noting "there's a substantial amount of evidence to support the jury's finding, which is why I was prepared to dismiss on the spot." OpenAI lead attorney William Savitt: "It's not a technical decision, it's a substantive one. It says: You brought your claims too late, and you did it because you were sitting on them to use them as a weapon of a competitor who can't compete in the marketplace." Musk on X called it a "calendar technicality" and announced an appeal to the 9th Circuit. Remedies that are now barred: >$130B disgorgement to OpenAI's nonprofit arm; removal of Altman and Brockman; unwinding of OpenAI's for-profit conversion (the structure that supports the company's ~$500B–$850B valuation and the >$130B in committed investor capital from Microsoft, SoftBank, Nvidia and Amazon).
