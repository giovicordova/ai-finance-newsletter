## Notes (archive only — not sent to Telegram)

### Stories considered
- **Databricks raising a strategic round at a $188 billion valuation — chosen for the news post.** Announced Thursday 16 July 2026 via Databricks' own newsroom press release (the primary source; the deal was first reported by the Wall Street Journal and corroborated by TechCrunch, Reuters via Yahoo Finance, SiliconANGLE, PYMNTS and US News). Databricks said it has signed a term sheet for a strategic funding round that values the company at $188 billion, led by existing investor Coatue, with additional new and existing investors, and expects to close later this summer. Secondary reporting (WSJ, Reuters) puts the round size at ~$3 billion and frames the $188B as roughly a 40% jump from the ~$134 billion valuation set in a ~$5 billion raise earlier in 2026 (some outlets date that prior mark to February, others to a December reference — the exact prior-round date is reported inconsistently). The company says the capital will fund its AI strategy — the Unity AI Gateway (multi-model governance), Genie (an AI data agent) and Lakebase (a serverless Postgres database for AI agents) — plus future AI acquisitions and research. This is the freshest, largest, cleanly free-primary-sourceable AI-finance transaction in the window: one of the highest valuations ever put on a private AI company, and a fresh angle for the channel, which has run heavy on the chip-supplier layer all week (SK Hynix, Helsing, Nebius, ASML, TSMC). **The news post links the Databricks primary and sticks to what that release clearly supports** — the $188 billion valuation, Coatue leading, and the expected summer close. The ~$3 billion round size and the prior-valuation comparison originate in WSJ/Reuters reporting (WSJ paywalled), so they are held in these Notes rather than claimed against the primary, and the softer "term sheet / signed" framing is kept out of the post to avoid over-claiming a closed deal. [https://www.databricks.com/company/newsroom/press-releases/databricks-raising-strategic-round-funding-188-billion-valuation]

Money-moving and on-theme, but preliminary/proposed talks — not a discrete, dated, executed transaction — so too soft to lead a factual news post. ### Pattern context
The channel spent last week almost entirely on the pick-and-shovel layer that gates AI hardware — SK Hynix's record listing (13 Jul), Helsing's institutionally backed round (14 Jul), Nebius/Reflection's compute pre-commitment (15 Jul), ASML's guidance raise (16 Jul) and TSMC's record profit and raised capex (17 Jul). Today moves up the stack to the software/data layer: Databricks, a private company, is being marked at $188 billion — one of the largest private AI valuations on record — while the chip suppliers were posting audited numbers. The counter-current the week surfaced (BIS warnings, AI bond-market fatigue) still frames the open question of whether these private marks and the hardware capex can be sustained; Big Tech earnings on 22–30 July are the next test, with Alphabet first on 22 July.
