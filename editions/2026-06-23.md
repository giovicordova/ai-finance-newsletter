### Stories considered
- **Dell's PowerEdge XE8812 launch and the Doudna DOE supercomputer — chosen for the news post.** Dell Technologies introduced the PowerEdge XE8812 on Monday 22 June at the ISC High Performance 2026 conference in Hamburg — the genuinely-fresh, in-window AI-infrastructure beat. The server is built on Nvidia's next-generation Vera Rubin NVL4 architecture and scales to 144 graphics processors per rack inside Dell's PowerRack 9100. The money/positioning facts: Dell, Nvidia and the U.S. Department of Energy's NERSC are using the XE8812 to build "Doudna," the next flagship national supercomputer at Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory (named for Nobel laureate Jennifer Doudna; expected ~10x the performance of the current Perlmutter system, with Nvidia Quantum-X800 InfiniBand networking); Dell also reported that more than 5,000 customers have deployed its AI Factory systems globally (per an April 2026 order-data analysis); the XE8812 is globally available early next year. The news post sticks to claims Dell's own IR release supports — the Vera Rubin-based server, the Dell/Nvidia/DOE Doudna build at Lawrence Berkeley, and the 5,000-customer figure — and omits the per-rack GPU count, the Perlmutter comparison and the share-price reaction, which are spec detail or secondary framing. https://investors.delltechnologies.com/news-releases/news-release-details/dell-ai-factory-nvidia-advances-supercomputing-class
- **Super Micro Computer's matching Vera Rubin NVL4 blueprint — runner-up, same event.** Supermicro introduced its Data Center Building Block Solutions Blueprint for HPC on the same Nvidia Vera Rubin NVL4 platform at ISC 2026, a liquid-cooled design scaling to 1,152 Rubin graphics processors and 576 Vera CPUs per scalable unit, with systems expected in Q4 2026. The same supply-side story as Dell, but the Dell release carries the more concrete money/positioning fact (a named U.S. government flagship deployment plus the 5,000-customer figure), so Supermicro is the runner-up. https://ir.supermicro.com/news/news-details/2026/Supermicro-Delivers-NVIDIA-Vera-Rubin-NVL4-End-to-End-DCBBS-Blueprint-with-Native-FP64-Performance-for-Converged-HPC-and-AI-Infrastructure/default.aspx
