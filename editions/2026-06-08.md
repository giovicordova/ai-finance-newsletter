### Stories considered
- Trump–OpenAI direct government equity stake talks — chosen for news post. President Trump told reporters aboard Air Force One on Friday 5 June 2026 that the federal government may take direct ownership stakes in leading AI companies, naming OpenAI, Anthropic and xAI; CNBC confirmed Friday that Sam Altman and the White House have been in active talks for over a year about an arrangement under which OpenAI would donate equity (not sell it) to seed the "Public Wealth Fund" outlined in OpenAI's April 2026 policy proposal. Trump's exact quote: "There are concepts where pieces could be given to the American public, where the American public essentially becomes a partner." OpenAI is privately valued above $850 billion and is preparing a potential IPO as soon as this year. Context: the Trump administration has already taken equity stakes in Intel, IBM and other quantum and critical-mineral companies during the president's second term. Anthropic is specifically not in talks (carry-over from the February 2026 federal-agency ban after Anthropic declined Pentagon use without safety guardrails). Senator Bernie Sanders, separately, has called for the US government to acquire 50% equity in OpenAI, Anthropic, xAI and similar firms, plus a 50% tax on their stock.
