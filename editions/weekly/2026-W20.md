### Pattern context
This week the daily print cycle did something it has not done before in this archive: every layer of the AI capacity stack reported numbers within 72 hours, and every print accelerated rather than moderated. On the tooling layer, Applied Materials delivered record Q2 revenue, record EPS, the highest gross margin in 25 years, and — most importantly — raised its 2026 semi-equipment industry growth guide from "over 20%" (February) to "over 30%" (today), with the Q3 revenue guide $860M above consensus. On the pure-play silicon layer, Cerebras priced its $5.55B IPO at $185 (Wed evening) and debuted Thursday at $350, closing +68% at $311 for a ~$95B market cap — the largest US tech IPO since Uber 2019. On the enterprise networking layer, Cisco beat Q3 estimates and raised its FY26 AI-orders outlook to ~$9B from $5B against $5.3B already booked YTD, while cutting 4,000 jobs — the third profitable, growing public company in two weeks (after Cloudflare and Block-style precedents) to take headcount down for an AI pivot. On the China hyperscaler layer, Tencent's capex hit $4.4B (+63% QoQ) and AI spend is doubling to RMB36B+ in 2026 with buybacks scaled back to fund it; Alibaba posted its first operating loss since the 2021 Covid trough as CEO Eddie Wu prioritized AI burn over near-term margin. The buildout's demand side is now being printed across every measurement surface (tooling guides, IPO valuations, FY order books, hyperscaler capex) at the same time, and at every measurement surface the slope is steeper than the previous-quarter projection.
