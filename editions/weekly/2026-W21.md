### Pattern context
Four set-pieces sequenced in five trading days, each removing a structural concern the bears had been pricing in: (1) the legal overhang on OpenAI's corporate form (Mon Musk verdict), (2) the supply-side concentration risk that Nvidia was the only viable AI-chip merchant cloud (Mon-Tue Blackstone-Google TPU JV), (3) the demand-side question of whether AI compute revenue could keep compounding without China (Wed-Thu Nvidia FY27 Q1 print with $91B guide and zero China), (4) the exit-path question of whether the largest private AI labs could ever get to public markets while litigation and structure were unresolved (Thu-Fri OpenAI confidential IPO filing). Recurring names tightening across the week: OpenAI, Nvidia, Alphabet/Google, Blackstone, Microsoft, SoftBank (Tokyo +20% Thursday), Anthropic (still pending raise). Next-week threads to watch: confidential-filing process timing for OpenAI (typical confidential-to-public S-1 window is ~2 months, pointing toward a late-July/early-August public S-1), Anthropic's reported $30B-$50B raise close, SpaceX S-1 amendments, and whether Nvidia's $91B Q2 guide pulls forward any 2027 demand pull-ins from hyperscaler capex commentary.
