### Pattern context
Friday's beat shifts the week's dominant thread from supply to demand. The week ran almost entirely on how the AI compute build-out is being financed and who books the revenue: SpaceX's record equity listing (>$2T debut, Monday), Nvidia's first bond sale since 2021 priced at $25 billion and ~3.4x oversubscribed (Tuesday/Wednesday), Jabil's raised AI-revenue outlook as capex lands on a picks-and-shovels supplier (Thursday), with SpaceX's $60B all-stock purchase of Cursor's parent running alongside as the AI-M&A turn. Today's Accenture print is the first read on the *demand* side: the world's largest technology-consulting firm grew 6% and pointed to large-scale AI-transformation work, yet new bookings slipped to $19.3B from $19.7B — the buyer of AI services showing softer order flow even as the sellers of AI infrastructure boom. Recurring names: Nvidia, SpaceX/xAI, OpenAI, Anthropic, Jabil, Accenture. Friday's take synthesises the supply-roaring / demand-uncertain divergence.

- **Capex becoming someone else's revenue** — the build-out's spending showed up as booked supplier revenue at Jabil, which raised its AI-revenue outlook ([18 June](https://github.com/giovicordova/ai-finance-newsletter/blob/main/editions/2026-06-18.md)); the picks-and-shovels layer is the early, visible beneficiary.
- **Supply roaring, demand the open question** — Friday's Accenture print (revenue +6%, but new bookings down to $19.3B from $19.7B) is the first sign the enterprise-demand side that's meant to turn all this capex into productivity is not accelerating in lockstep with the infrastructure spend.
