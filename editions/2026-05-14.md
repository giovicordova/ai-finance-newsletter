### Pattern context
This week the multi-layer capacity thread keeps tightening at every layer of the stack. Monday: Tower Semi disclosed $1.3B in 2027 silicon-photonics contracts (the same photonics layer Nvidia underwrote at Corning last week). Tuesday evening: Isomorphic Labs' $2.1B Series B brought sovereign capital (MGX, Temasek, the UK Sovereign AI Fund) alongside Thrive, Alphabet, GV and CapitalG into AI-native drug discovery — Thrive having also led OpenAI's largest private rounds. Tonight: Cerebras priced at $185 — $25 above the upwardly revised range, after three pricing hikes from $115–$125 → $150–$160 → $185, with the order book reportedly ~20x oversubscribed and fully diluted valuation near $56B. It's one of the largest US tech IPOs on record, in a year in which Nvidia is already directly capitalising suppliers (Corning, CoreWeave, IREN) and sovereign vehicles are writing nine-figure cheques into application-layer AI-natives. Cerebras is the first 2026 pure-play AI silicon name to clear the public-market test at full size, and it does so a week before Nvidia prints fiscal Q1 — the demand side of the same thread.
