AI FINANCE BRIEFING — 15 Apr 2026
═══════════════════════════════════════════

ASML lifted its 2026 sales ceiling to €40B this morning — the single most reliable gauge of AI capex just flashed acceleration, not exhaustion.

── FIVE THINGS ─────────────────────────
1. **ASML raises full-year guidance as AI orders keep climbing** — Q1 net sales €8.8B, gross margin 53%, net income €2.8B. Management pushed the 2026 range from €34–39B to €36–40B, citing EUV demand led by TSMC and "logic customers" expanding 3nm/2nm capacity. This is the second up-guide in three quarters from the company that sits upstream of every frontier model.
   → https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/15/asml-q1-2026-earnings-report.html

2. **JPMorgan guides 2026 expenses to $105B, up $9B on AI and branches** — Net income $16.5B vs $14.6B a year ago, but the headline was the expense trajectory: $105B planned for 2026 versus $96B last year, with AI automation named as a primary driver. Dimon's bank has the biggest AI talent bench of any global bank (Evident Insights) — and just put a price tag on it.
   → https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/14/jpmorgan-chase-jpm-earnings-1q-2026.html

3. **Bank of America prints Q1 this morning, Agentic AI on the call** — Consensus EPS ~$1.00, record client balances of $4.1T. Moynihan has pre-committed the efficiency ratio to 60% with Erica 2.0 and agentic advisory doing the heavy lifting. First read on whether the AI-efficiency story survives contact with operating costs.
   → https://www.financialcontent.com/article/marketminute-2026-4-7-bank-of-america-q1-earnings-preview-focus-on-nii-and-fee-growth

4. **OpenAI acquires Hiro Finance in vertical personal-finance push** — The acqui-hire, announced yesterday, brings an autonomous personal-finance reasoning team inside OpenAI — the second vertical acqui-hire of the month across the frontier labs (Anthropic took Coefficient Bio at $400M on 4/3). Frontier-lab strategy is visibly shifting from horizontal API to vertical product.
   → https://www.financialcontent.com/article/marketminute-2026-4-14-openai-acquires-hiro-finance-the-push-into-vertical-ai-personal-finance

5. **Anthropic at $30B ARR vs OpenAI $25B — OpenAI's CRO disputes $8B of it** — SaaStr confirms Anthropic's revenue run-rate crossed OpenAI's for the first time, on an 80% enterprise mix. OpenAI is telling staff the real figure is closer to $22B. The revenue-recognition war between the two labs is now a live debate that IPO bankers will have to adjudicate.
   → https://www.saastr.com/anthropic-just-passed-openai-in-revenue-while-spending-4x-less-to-train-their-models/

── DEEP DIVE ───────────────────────────
ASML's up-guide is the most important data point of the week because it sits directly against the Great Rotation we've been tracking since 3/27. Public markets have spent three weeks punishing AI equities: Microsoft down 24% in Q1 (4/4), the IPO window closed (4/1), Wellington publicly pivoting from AI exposure. The narrative said monetisation was lagging capex and the cycle had peaked. Then the one company that can't fake its order book raised the ceiling.

ASML's read is more reliable than any hyperscaler guide because its customers — TSMC, Samsung, Intel — bid for EUV slots 18–24 months ahead of production. You don't add €1B to the top of your annual guide unless the backlog has firmed up that much in the last ninety days. TSMC, which reports tomorrow and already pre-announced $35.7B in Q1 revenue with $56B of 2026 capex, is the largest EUV buyer. ASML raising implies TSMC is about to ratify its capex guide with orders, not hope.

Who wins: EUV and advanced-packaging suppliers — ASML, Applied Materials, TEL, ASM International. Marvell (NVIDIA's photonics partner per 4/7) gets a second-order lift as photonics becomes the binding constraint inside those new fabs. Who loses: the rotation trade. Anyone short the cycle on the thesis that MSFT's 24% drop was a leading indicator just had their upstream data point go the other way.

What's priced in: mean-reversion from the March sell-off. What's missed: public-equity weakness and infrastructure demand have decoupled entirely. The ROI question at the application layer doesn't dent the 18-month-forward order book at the foundation layer. That's not a bubble signal — it's a signal the constraint has moved to the one place the market can't rerate quickly: physical capacity.

── TRAJECTORY ──────────────────────────
Three weeks of deep dives have tracked a market splitting into two realities: private capital pouring into AI (Q1's $242B, 4/3) while public equities rotate out (MSFT's 24% drop, 4/4; the closed IPO window, 4/1). ASML adds a third axis — infrastructure demand is still accelerating upstream regardless of what public multiples do. The constraint stack we've mapped (GPUs → CPUs → memory → energy → fabs → photonics) keeps hardening. Meanwhile JPM's $105B expense guide introduces a new thread: the cost of AI is arriving on the income statements of its customers, not just its builders.

── ONE CONTRARIAN THOUGHT ──────────────
The consensus read on BAC at 8:30 this morning will be that Erica 2.0 and agentic advisory are "driving efficiency." I'd read the tape the other way. JPM just guided 2026 expenses up $9B with AI automation named as the driver. Citi and Wells have been quieter but their cost bases are moving the same direction. Banks spent 2024–25 selling AI to shareholders as a cost-cut story; in 2026 it's arriving as a capex story wearing an opex costume. The efficiency ratios will get worse before they get better, and the market will punish the first bank that admits it openly. Watch which CFO breaks cover first.
