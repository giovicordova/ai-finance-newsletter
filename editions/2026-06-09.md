### Pattern context
Yesterday's edition explicitly flagged this story as "held for tomorrow if the licensing economics print on stage as previewed" — they did. Today's beat closes that thread and opens a bigger one. Apple's choice is structurally distinct from every other frontier-AI placement of capital this year: where OpenAI, Anthropic and xAI are being courted by both the Trump administration (for direct equity stakes) and private-credit balance sheets (Apollo / Blackstone for Anthropic's $36B TPU SPV), Apple did not buy or build — it leased. A custom-trained 1.2T-parameter Gemini for ~$1B/year, hosted on Google's cloud, on Nvidia GPUs Apple does not own. That makes Google the de facto AI brain for ~2B Apple devices, and routes a meaningful slice of Apple's future inference spend through a competitor's data centres. Recurring names this week: Apple, Google, Nvidia, OpenAI, Anthropic, Sam Altman, Trump, SpaceX, DeepSeek, Apollo, Blackstone, Tim Cook (now exiting), John Ternus (incoming CEO Sept 1). Worth watching for the rest of the week: SpaceX prices Thursday and debuts Friday — that's the capital-markets event that closes the week. Anthropic's $36B private-credit SPV remains the dominant non-equity AI infrastructure-financing story; any closing-day printing will be Friday-take material. The Apple–Google licensing model is now a live alternative blueprint to the equity-and-debt model that's been dominant for OpenAI and Anthropic — Friday's weekly take should set those two financing patterns side by side.
